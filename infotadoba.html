Tadoba National Park

Tadoba National Park is located in the Indian state of Maharashtra. It was established in 1955 and covers an area of 1727 square kilometers.

The park is known for its population of Bengal tigers, which can be seen on safari tours. Other wildlife includes Indian leopards, sloth bears, and Indian bison.

Visitors can also enjoy birdwatching, with over 200 species of birds recorded in the park.

Accommodation options include lodges and campsites within the park.
